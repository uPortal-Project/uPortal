    Using your web e-mail  


    Logging in to myUBC email


    If are using the mail from the guest page, login the mail channel using your Netinfo/Interchange username and password.


    If you login to myUBC using your student ID and pin, you will need to login to the mail channel using your Netinfo/Interchange username and password. The mail channel will not automatically login for your own security.


    [Return to top] 


    myUBC mail channel

     

    Upon login in to myUBC, your email channel will list the number of new messages received and give you two option to enter the program. 

    • Clicking on "Read Messages" will take you to the Inbox folder where new messages are stored.

    • Clicking on "Compose"  will take allow you to start composing a new message immediately.

    [Return to top] 



    Receiving Mail 

    Reading mail

    New messages will be stored in the Inbox folder. You can reach the Inbox folder by selecting the "Read Messages" button on the web mail channel after you login into the portal. To check for new messages select the "Check for new mail" in the Inbox. The program automatically checks for new messages every time you leave the Inbox and return. Ie: You open a message or another folder and return to the Inbox. 


    Your messages are displayed in a list format, which shows if the message is new (in yellow), who it's from, the subject, the date/time, and size of the message. The default is that it will show the bottom of the list first (with the newest messages). You can re-sort your list by clicking on the column title; i.e. clicking on "Date/Time" would sort the messages by date/time received.


    To read a message in your Inbox folder select the message upon entering the program and click on the message Subject. The program opens the selected email message and displays it for you to read.


    To read a message saved in another folders, select "Folders" from the top buttons. Select the folder where the message is stored by clicking on the Folder name. Click on the message Subject you wish to read.


    [Return to top]


    Mail Functions 


    When finished reading your mail, you can perform any of the following functions by using the appropriate features located at the top or bottom of your open message. Following is the definition of each function:


    Reply Click the "Reply" button to reply to the sender of the message
    Reply All Click the "Reply All" button to reply to the sender and all recipients of the original message.
    Forward Click the "Forward" button to forward the message to another email address. 
    Delete Click the "Delete" button to delete the current message. Your deleted message will be saved in your Wastebasket folder.
    Return to folder This return you to the folder where the message is stored.
    Next message Opens the next message in the folder. When there are no more messages it returns you to the folder. Use the "Back" button on your browser to return to a previous message or return to the folder and re-select the message.
    Print Click File/Print on your browser to print your message.

    Receiving and Saving Attachments 


    If a link appears at the top right of a message you have received, there is a file attached to the message. To retrieve the attachment, please follow these steps: 

    • Click the hypertext attachment link.

    • At this point, your Web browser displays your attachment or allows you to save the attached file to your hard drive. If you must save the file, follow these steps: 

      • Click the "Save File" button and designate where you would like the file saved on your hard drive.

      • See your Web browser's online Help files for more information on your save options.

      • To read your attached file, locate it on your computer's hard drive and open it.

    • If you would like to save your attachment without opening it, click on the link using the right button on your mouse. Follow the windows to save it to your hard drive.

    Composing Mail


    Sending mail


    To compose and send mail  please follow these steps:

    • Select "Compose" from the myUBC mail channel OR

    • Click "Compose" from the top command bar from anywhere in the program.

    • Fill in the header information (See Addressing your messages).

    • Compose your message in the large message area provided. 

    • Click the "Send" button at the top or bottom of the screen. 

    [Return to top] 


    Addressing your messages

     

    Type in the information below as necessary. For multiple recipient separate with a comma between addresses.


    To Type in the recipient's email address.
    CC Type in any additional recipients' email addresses. A copy of your email will be sent to them.
    BCC Type in the email address of any recipient that you would like to send a copy to without any other recipient's knowledge.
    Subj Type in the subject of the message.


    [Return to top] 


    Saving an email as a draft


    "Save as draft" allows you to save a draft of your message and finish composing and sending it later. Simply click the "Save as draft" button to save a draft message for sending at a later time. When you're ready to continue your message, open your "Draft" folder, select the message and resume composing.

    Saving copies in the sent folder


    Currently a copy of all messages sent are saved in the "Sent Messages" folder. Please maintain/clean your "Sent Messages" folder on a regular basis.


    [Return to top] 


    Sending attachments


    You can only send a maximum of 5 attachments per message. The total size of the message, including attachments cannot be larger then 3 megabytes. To add an attachment to a message, follow the steps below:

    • Open the message to which you would like to add an attachment.

    • On the right of the compose area, under Attachments select one of the "Browse" buttons.

    • Select the file on your computer/ disk/ network and click "Open".

    • Your file is attached to the message.

    • You can repeat this process to add more attachments to the message.

    When the message is sent, the attachment will be sent.

    Moving/ Deleting Messages

    Other functionalities for messages include moving messages between folders and deleting messages. 

    Moving message(s), follow the steps below:

    • Open the folder where the message is currently stored.

    • Select one or more messages to be moved by checking on the box next (to the left) of the message.

    • Select from the drop down folder list "Choose Folder", at the top of the email list, which folder the messages will be moved to.

    • Click "Move"

    Deleting message(s), follow the steps below:

    • Open the folder where the message is currently stored.

    • Select one or more messages to be deleted by checking on the box next (to the left) of the message.

    • Click "Delete". Deleted messages are moved to the Wastebasket folder.

    • To permanently delete a message you must empty the Wastebasket folder.

      • Go to "Folders"

      • select "Empty Wastebasket". All messages in the folder will be permanently deleted.

    Folders


    Creating a folder


    To create a folder follow the steps below:

    • Open the Folders page, by clicking "Folders" on the top command bar.

    • Click "New"

    • Enter the name for your new folder. Click "Create".

    • Your new folder will be available in the "Folders" list.

    Note: If you try to create a folder with an existing folder name, the program will not complete the operation. The folder will not be created.

    [Return to top] 


    Renaming a folder


    To rename a folder follow the steps below:

    • Open the Folders page, by clicking "Folders" on the top command bar.

    • Select the folder to rename by checking the box next to the folder name.

    • Click "Rename"

    • Enter the new name for your folder. Click "Rename".

    • Your renamed folder will be available in the "Folders" list.

    Note: If you try to rename a folder to an existing folder name, the program will not complete the operation. The folder will keep it's current name.


    [Return to top] 


    Deleting a folder


    To delete a folder follow the steps below:

    • Open the Folders page, by clicking "Folders" on the top command bar.

    • Select the folder to be deleted by checking the box next to the folder name.

    • Click "Delete"

    • The folder will be permanently deleted. All the messages associated with the folder will also be deleted.

    How can I minimize junk mail?


    The following measures can be taken to minimize the amount of spam (junk mail) received in your myUBC email account.

    • NEVER reply to a spam email or ask to be taken off the distribution list — doing so only confirms that your email address is of a valid person. 

    • Whenever you are asked to provide your email address to a company or Web site (e.g. product registration, surveys, etc.) make sure there is a privacy policy posted and that the policy explicitly states that your email address will not be provided or sold to third parties.

    If you have an Interchange account, go to www.interchange.ubc.ca ,select "For Users", "My account" and "E-mail filtering". You will be able to block specific addresses from spaming your account.

    Changing font or font size on your browser 


    Your browser setting may need to be tuned to view myUBC mail. One of the most common issue is the browser font size, you may want to adjust your screen to fully use your screen. Below are the instructions to change the font size in the two most common browsers. 

     

    Netscape Communicator

    To change the font and font size: 

    • Open your browser.

    • Select Preferences on the Edit menu.

    • Click Fonts under the Appearances category.

    • Choose a new font and font size from the pull-down menus.

    • Click the "Ok" button.

     

    Internet Explorer


    To change the font size:

    • Open your browser.

    • Select Fonts on the View menu.

    • Choose the font size you prefer from the pull-down menu.

    To change the font: 

    • Open your browser. 

    • Select Internet Options on the View menu.

    • Click Fonts on the General tab.

    • Choose a new font from the pull-down menus.

    • Click the "Ok" button.
